What is Animation?

"The process of making the illusion of motion and the illusion of change by means of rapid succession of sequential images that minimally differ from each other" Wikipedia.
In short, it's when an artist puts images, traditionally hand drawn, one after another to make it seem as though the connected images are one moving piece. This can be used in a number of ways, such as; film, tv and video games, which is the form people are most familiar with, but also in motion graphics which are often used in marketing and creative arts and many other areas. But in relation to the internet, animation is used in so many things; GIFs, popups (i.e. transitioning from images to text or adding or removing items from a list), certain animations to guide users through an interface, animating a subject or object to help tell a story and much more... On the web, this is achieved through a number of different technologies and techniques. Click the links at the top of the page to learn more about some of these.
