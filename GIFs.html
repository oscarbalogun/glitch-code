Gifs (Graphics Interchange Format) were created by Steve Wilhite and his team at Compuserve in 1987, as a way to share colour image files without using up too much of a computers memory. Initially it was just used for still images but a compression algorithm, named Lempel-Ziv-Welch after its creators Abraham Lemepl, Jacob Ziv and Terry Welch, allowed the files to hold multiple variations of a still image which could be put together to create a looping video.
