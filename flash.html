In 1996 Adobe Flash was released. a multimedia software used for animation, internet and desktop applications and mobile apps and games. Unlike GIFs, Flash allowed for audio and video as well as input from mouse, keyboard, microphone and camera. This made it perfect for animation and web games.

Flash Animation

Adobe had multiple software that allowed creators to make all sorts of media on flash. For animation, for example, artists could use Adobe Flash Professional (now Adobe Animate) to make animations. It was even used to make a number of cartoons and animated films. The first being Ren and Stimpy created by John Kricfalusi and released in 1997, it was the first of many cartoons produced specifically for the internet.

In 1999, David B Williams and his studio, Visionary media, released Whirlgirl. A cartoon about a super-heroine fighting for freedom against a futuristic mediatech empire. This was a big deal and a major milestone for Adobe Animations as it premiered on the cable channel, Showtime, and released at the same time on the showtime website, making it the first regularly scheduled Flash Animated series.

Since then Flash Animation has been used for a number of different shows and feature films and Adobe Animate is even still used today for many animated films and tv shows.

Decline of flash

The birth of mobile meant that flash started to be used less and less on the web as the extensive cpu that flash files produced as they were processed drained the battery too fast for early mobile devices. Because of this apple decided not to support flash on any of their devices so developers eventually stopped using it and the internet went back to being static again (apart from GIFs) until the introduction of CSS.
